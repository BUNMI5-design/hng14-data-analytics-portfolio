## Project Overview

This project analysed 15.7 million tweets about climate change posted between 2006 and 2019 to uncover how public sentiment and discourse evolved over 14 years. The analysis covered stance detection, aggressiveness patterns, regional discourse and the impact of major global events on climate conversation.

The final deliverable was a 3-page interactive Power BI dashboard enabling non-technical stakeholders to explore sentiment trends without writing a single query.

## Key Findings

**Sentiment Shift**
Climate believers grew from 16.67 percent in 2006 to 79.40 percent by 2019 — a dramatic 62.73 percentage point shift over 14 years.

**Aggressiveness**
Climate deniers were 42.65 percent more aggressive in their language than climate believers across the entire dataset.

**Paris Agreement Impact**
The Paris Agreement signing in 2015 caused the largest single-year sentiment shift in the entire 14-year dataset, with believer tweets spiking significantly.

**Global Impact**
2.38 billion people were affected by climate-related disasters during the same period covered by the dataset.

**Engagement Patterns**
Tweets with hashtags generated significantly higher engagement than those without, suggesting that organised climate campaigns drove the majority of discourse volume.
